### Strategy for Manual Removal
Since you have to do this manually, use it to your advantage:
1.  **Batch Process:** Do them all at once on a Friday afternoon or Monday morning.
2.  **The "Personal Touch":** Because you are doing it manually, you can actually send a personal DM on TradingView.
    *   *Message:* "Hey [Username], just auditing the access list. Saw your sub was cancelled so I have to remove the access today. Hope the tools served you well! If you ever want back in, hit me up for a discount code. Cheers, Garry."
3.  **Why this works:** It's not a "bot" banning them. It's a human. They might reply: "Oh wait, my card expired! I didn't mean to cancel! How do I pay?" -> **Boom, recovered sale.**

### Where to manage this list?
Create a simple Excel/Google Sheet:
*   **Column A:** TradingView Username
*   **Column B:** Expiry Date
*   **Column C:** Status (Active / Cancelled / Warning Sent / Removed)

Don't just delete them. Mark them as "Removed" so you have a list of "warm leads" to email in 3 months with a "We miss you" offer.
